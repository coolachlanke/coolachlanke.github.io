Understanding Your Computer

CPU (Central Processing Unit)

  • Often considered the "brain" of the computer, the CPU handles most calculations which enable your computer to function.

RAM (Random Access Memory)

  • RAM is your system's short-term memory. It temporarily stores data that your computer is actively using so that it can be accessed quickly.

Storage (HDD/SSD)

  • HDDs (Hard Disk Drives) and SSDs (Solid State Drives) are the primary means of long-term data storage in computers. SSDs are faster but often more expensive per GB than HDDs.

GPU (Graphics Processing Unit)

  • The GPU is specialized hardware for rendering images and videos. It's particularly important for gaming, video editing, and rendering 3D graphics.

Motherboard

  • The motherboard is the main circuit board that connects all the different parts of the computer together, including the CPU, RAM, storage, and GPU.

Power Supply

  • This component converts power from your home electricity supply into a form that the computer components can use.
